Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 16, 2026

This policy explains what oneTap ("the app," "we," "us") does and does not do with your information. oneTap is currently in development and has not yet been published; this policy will be kept up to date as the app is finalized and released.

The short version

oneTap has no user accounts, no login, and no advertising or analytics tracking. It works almost entirely on your device. The one thing that leaves your device is the text you paste, share, or speak into oneTap when you ask it to figure out a calendar event, alarm, or reminder — that text is sent to a third-party AI service to be interpreted, and is not stored on our own servers, because we don't operate any.

Information we process

1. Text you paste, share, or speak

When you paste text, share text into oneTap from another app, or use a voice command that isn't a simple device control (see below), that text is sent to a third-party AI language model provider so it can be turned into structured event details (title, date, time, duration, and similar fields). This text may include names, places, or other personal details if you included them in the message you provided.

  • We do not store a copy of this text on any server we operate — oneTap doesn't have a backend of its own.
  • The AI provider processes the request to generate a response and is contractually a data processor for this purpose; it is not used to build a profile of you, and is not shared for advertising.
  • We may change AI providers over time as the app evolves; this policy will be updated to reflect that.

2. Calendar and alarm data

With your permission, oneTap reads and writes calendar events (to create or remove events you've asked it to act on) and creates device alarms. This all happens directly through Android's own calendar and alarm system on your device — oneTap does not copy this data anywhere else. If your device's calendar account is itself synced to a service (for example, a Google account you've added to your phone), that sync is handled by your device/account provider, not by oneTap.

oneTap keeps a small local record (on your device only) of which calendar events it created, so it can find and remove the right one later if you ask it to cancel something.

3. Voice commands and microphone access

Voice commands are opt-in and only listen while you're actively tapping-to-talk — oneTap does not listen in the background or continuously. Speech-to-text conversion is handled by your device's built-in speech recognition service (the same one used elsewhere on Android), not by oneTap's own infrastructure.

A small, fixed set of device-control commands (volume, brightness, flashlight, media playback, ringer mode, Do Not Disturb) are matched entirely on your device and never leave it. Anything else you say that isn't one of these commands is treated the same as pasted/shared text, per the section above.

4. Device settings permissions

Voice commands can, at your request, change a few device settings directly: media volume, screen brightness, flashlight, ringer mode, and Do Not Disturb. These changes happen locally through Android's own system APIs and involve no data collection — we simply change the setting you asked for.

5. App preferences

Simple preferences — like whether the floating bubble or voice commands are turned on, and where you last positioned the bubble on screen — are stored locally on your device and are never transmitted anywhere.

6. Google Drive backup (optional)

oneTap can optionally back up your transactions, calendar events, and alarm history to your own Google Drive account, so you don't lose that data if you switch or lose your phone. This is entirely manual and opt-in — nothing is backed up unless you tap "Connect Google Drive" and then "Back up now" yourself.

  • oneTap only ever requests the drive.appdata permission scope, which gives it access to a single hidden folder created for oneTap specifically — not your wider Google Drive, and not any of your other files.
  • This hidden folder isn't visible in Google Drive's normal website or app, even to you — it exists solely so oneTap can store and retrieve its own backup file.
  • The backup file contains the same transaction, event, and alarm-history data described above, and nothing else (no account passwords, no Google account data beyond what's needed to store the file).
  • We (the developer) never see this data — it goes directly from your device to your own Google Drive over Google's servers, the same way any other app you authorize to use Google Drive works.
  • Tapping "Disconnect" in Settings stops oneTap from accessing Drive going forward. Fully removing the app's stored backup data is done the same way as for any other app you've connected to your Google account: revoking oneTap's access from your Google Account's connected-apps/permissions settings removes its hidden data folder, or you can use "Restore" first if you want that data back on the device before removing it from Drive.

What we don't do

  • No account creation, sign-in, or password of any kind.
  • No advertising, ad networks, or ad tracking.
  • No analytics or crash-reporting SDKs that build a profile of you.
  • No selling or renting of any information to third parties.

Data retention and deletion

Because oneTap stores almost everything locally on your device, uninstalling the app (or clearing its storage in Android's app settings) removes its local data, including preferences and the record of app-created calendar events. Text sent to the AI provider for interpretation is processed to generate a response and is not retained by us, since we don't operate a server to retain it on. If you have questions about a specific request, contact us using the email below.

Settings also has a "Reset app" option you can use any time, which immediately clears every transaction, event, and alarm history entry saved locally on your device and returns you to the first-run walkthrough. It only ever touches local data — it does not delete, alter, or disconnect anything already saved in your own Google Drive backup (see above).

If you've used the optional Google Drive backup feature, see "Google Drive backup" above for how to remove that data — it lives in your own Google Drive account, not on any server we operate, so it's removed the same way you'd remove any other app's access to your Google account.

Children's privacy

oneTap is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect information from children under that age.

Changes to this policy

If oneTap's features or data handling change — for example, switching AI providers, or adding new voice commands — this page will be updated and the "Last updated" date above will change accordingly.
